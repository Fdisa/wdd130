Traditions

Mexico is a land full of magic and traditions. Over the years, people worldwide have been fascinated with the culture in Mexico. Visiting Mexico, regardless of your stay, is an excellent opportunity to explore the marvels deeply that a country like Mexico can offer you. Experiences that will remain in your memory forever.

Dia de Muertos

Day of the dead is a celebration on November 1 and 2. The belief is people who passed away come on these days to visit their families and closest ones. They also eat and enjoy these days of celebration. This is the reason why people in Mexico visit cemeteries, decorate tombs, put food on altars and pictures of their loved ones who passed away. People usually paint their faces in the shape of skulls to remember their ancestors. This is an unforgettable experience that anyone should lose.

Traditional Dances

Mexico is highly known for its diversity, and culture and dances are not an exception. Every state in Mexico has the privilege of sharing traditions through folkloric dances. The clothes used in every traditional dance can vary. White clothes, skirts, and hats are the traditional colors for dances in the south of Mexico, such as Veracruz. On the other hand, places like Jalisco use more vivid colors. Dances are a crucial aspect that defines Mexican culture.

Mexican Food

Mexican food is enough for travelers around the world to visit Mexico. Gastronomy in Mexico is diverse and delicious. The most popular dish is tacos; however, pozole, enchiladas, tostadas, tortas are other exquisite options. Using masa for many traditional dishes makes Mexican food simple, unique, and interesting for foreign travelers.
